# NFTones Brand Language One-Pager

For: founders, contractors, contributors, journalists, AI assistants, and anyone drafting NFTones copy.

If you're writing public-facing text for NFTones — a tweet, a landing page, a docs page, an email, an in-app modal — scan this sheet first. The rules below are non-negotiable.

---

## The launch promise

> **Free to start. Mint when sold.**

Use this exact line. Don't paraphrase it. It's load-bearing for the brand.

---

## What NFTones IS

- An artist ownership platform
- A direct-to-fan music economy
- A Solana-powered rights and royalty engine
- A creator-owned music infrastructure layer
- A music business platform that happens to use blockchain (not a blockchain platform that happens to support music)

## What NFTones IS NOT

- "Another NFT marketplace"
- A speculation platform
- A get-rich-quick crypto play
- A copyright office replacement
- A DRM / playback-restriction system
- A label

## $KTRS language — utility, never investment

**Always say:**
- "$KTRS powers the creator ecosystem"
- "$KTRS unlocks creator tools"
- "Use $KTRS for minting credits, AI tools, boosts, analytics, advanced rights"
- "Artists can buy, earn, or receive $KTRS"

**Never say:**
- "Buy $KTRS — price will rise"
- "Invest in $KTRS"
- "Guaranteed profit / returns"
- "Hold $KTRS to get rich"
- Anything about token price appreciation or speculation

If you're tempted to write a sentence about token price, delete it. There are no exceptions.

## Legal language — no copyright-replacement claims

**Safe (always use these):**
- "Proof of timestamp"
- "On-chain ownership record"
- "Rights declaration"
- "Royalty routing"
- "Licensing support"
- "Evidence package export"
- "Creator attestation"

**Unsafe (never use unless legally reviewed AND actually integrated):**
- "Guaranteed legal copyright protection"
- "Guaranteed court protection"
- "Guaranteed royalties forever"
- "Automatic copyright registration"
- "Replaces copyright office registration"
- "Legally bulletproof ownership"

If you're writing about what NFTones does for the artist legally, use the safe list. Always.

## Required artist attestation

Before any drop is published, the artist must check three boxes confirming:

1. "I confirm I own or have permission to monetize this audio."
2. "I understand NFTones provides timestamping, rights declarations, and royalty tools, but does not replace formal legal copyright registration."
3. "I understand false claims may result in takedown, account restriction, and forfeiture of platform privileges."

Don't change the wording without legal review.

---

## Onboarding rule — never gate behind $KTRS

Order is non-negotiable: free signup → upload → drop page → THEN surface $KTRS for premium tools. Artists must understand the value of NFTones before being asked to buy or earn the token.

**Bad:** "Buy $KTRS to unlock the platform"
**Good:** "Start free. Power up with $KTRS."

## Brand voice

Artist-first &middot; sovereign &middot; modern &middot; music-culture-aware &middot; Web3-enabled but never Web3-confusing &middot; premium but accessible &middot; creator-rebellion &middot; direct-to-fan energy

**Not:** scammy, corporate-dead, fake-hype, crypto-bro, condescending, jargon-heavy

When in doubt: would an indie rapper, beat maker, or vocalist read this and trust it? If no, rewrite.

## Visual direction (for designers)

Cyberpunk creator economy &middot; clean dark mode &middot; neon audio wave accents &middot; music studio dashboard energy &middot; animated waveform previews &middot; drop countdowns &middot; fan collector badges

Premium but accessible. Drop pages should feel viral and shareable. Artist profile pages should feel premium. Avoid generic crypto-app aesthetics (no diamonds, no rocket ships, no green-line price charts).

---

## What to hide from artists

Blockchain complexity should be invisible to non-crypto users. Wallet mechanics, gas fees, RPC endpoints, on-chain anchoring — all surface only as plain-English outcomes:

- ❌ "Anchor tx: 0x4f3a..."
- ✅ "Your release is registered."

- ❌ "Insufficient SOL for gas"
- ✅ "Mint cost is covered by the buyer at sale time."

Costs are always shown BEFORE action. Never surprise an artist with a fee.

## When in doubt

Default to the safe phrasing. Default to the artist's interest. Default to "free to start, mint when sold." Default to plain English over crypto jargon.

If a draft makes you uncertain, ask: "Would I send this to my friend who's an indie artist with no crypto background?" If you'd flinch, rewrite.
