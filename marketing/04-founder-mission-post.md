# Music Belongs to Creators Again

## Why I Built NFTones

A friend of mine sent me their Spotify payout last month. Real artist, real talent, real fan base. The check was $14. For ten thousand streams.

That's not an outlier. That's the system working exactly as designed.

Streaming platforms pay fractions of pennies. Labels take the masters. Middlemen take the payouts. The artist — the only person who actually made the thing — gets the smallest slice of every cycle.

I'm not the first person to notice this. I won't be the last. But I am building something about it.

## What NFTones is

NFTones is a Solana-powered platform where artists own their music, their drops, their royalties, and their fan relationships.

Not another NFT marketplace. Not a speculation play. Not a get-rich-quick crypto pitch.

Music ownership infrastructure. A direct-to-fan music economy. A Shopify-meets-Bandcamp built on rails that actually pay creators.

## What's actually different

Three things, and they're not negotiable.

**Artists own their masters.** Every release goes on-chain with the artist's wallet as the owner. Royalty splits route through smart contracts, not a label's accounting department. When you grant access to your music, you grant it. When you revoke, you revoke. No middleman gets to interpret your wishes.

**Free to start. Mint when sold.** I don't have the capital to subsidize free minting at scale, and I'm not going to lie to you about it. So we built lazy minting from day one. You upload your track free. You build your drop page free. The mint only happens when a fan actually buys — and the cost comes out of the sale or the buyer covers it. You don't pay to play.

**$KTRS powers the creator tools.** AI mastering. AI cover art. AI promo copy. Boosted drops. Advanced analytics. Those things cost real compute, and $KTRS is the credit you spend on them. You can buy, earn, or receive $KTRS credits. It's not an investment. It's not going to make you rich. It's the gas that runs the creator engine.

## Who this is for

Indie rappers. Beat makers. Producers. Vocalists. TikTok musicians. SoundCloud artists. Underground collectives. Small labels. Anyone who's ever felt like the music industry was built for everyone except the people making the music.

If your fans want to do more than stream — collect, support, unlock, sponsor your next drop — NFTones gives them the rails to do it. And every dollar that flows through is one you didn't have to beg an algorithm for.

## What this isn't

NFTones doesn't replace your copyright registration. It gives you on-chain timestamping, rights declarations, and royalty routing tools — for formal copyright protection, you still want to register with the copyright office.

NFTones doesn't promise your songs will go viral. It gives you the ownership layer underneath everything else. The hard work of building an audience is still yours.

NFTones doesn't tell you to buy a token to use it. You start free. Premium tools cost $KTRS, but you'll see the value before you ever spend a credit.

If that's you — or if you know someone it should be — reach out. The door is open.

Music belongs to creators again. Let's build it.
